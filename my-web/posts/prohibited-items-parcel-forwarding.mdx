Sending packages internationally from Poland is easier than ever with parcel forwarding services, but strict rules based on the latest regulations from major carriers like **FedEx**, **UPS**, and **DPD** prohibit or restrict certain items to ensure safety and customs compliance; ignoring these can lead to fines, delays, or confiscations.

![Commonly Prohibited Items](/img/blog/prohibitedItems/CommonlyProhibitedItems.webp)

## Commonly Prohibited Items

These categories represent goods that are **universally banned or heavily restricted** due to safety risks, legal controls, or import prohibitions in destination countries.

--

### ❌ Flammable and Hazardous Goods

![Hazardous Materials](/img/blog/prohibitedItems/FlammableLiquid.webp)

- **Aerosols, sprays, and pressurized containers**: Strictly prohibited in air and many ground shipments due to explosion risk.
- **Paints, solvents, adhesives, and chemicals**: Classified as dangerous goods under ADR and IATA regulations; require special packaging and permits if allowed at all.
- **Lighters, matches, fuel containers**: Forbidden due to fire hazard.
- **Powerbanks and lithium batteries (loose or unpackaged)**: Lithium-ion batteries pose fire risks; must comply with IATA packaging rules or are banned.
- **Corrosive substances and poisons**: Includes acids (hydrochloric, sulfuric), mercury, cyanide, and similar toxic materials.

--

### ❌ Perishable or Unlabeled Food Items

![Perishable Goods](/img/blog/prohibitedItems/FoodProducts.webp)

- **Fresh meat, dairy, cheese, fruits, vegetables**: Subject to strict phytosanitary and veterinary controls; often require import permits or are banned to prevent disease spread.
- **Homemade or unlabeled food**: Customs reject items without proper commercial labeling and ingredient disclosure.
- **Products requiring refrigeration**: Cannot be shipped without specialized cold chain logistics.

--

### ❌ Health and Controlled Products

![Health Products](/img/blog/prohibitedItems/HealthControlledProducts.webp)

- **Prescription medicines and controlled supplements**: Require prescriptions and import licenses; many are banned in parcels.
- **CBD, THC, hemp-based products**: Controlled substances with varying legality; usually prohibited without licenses.
- **Cosmetics with banned ingredients**: Some substances are restricted under EU and destination country safety laws.

--

### ❌ High-Risk or Valuable Items

![Valuable Items](/img/blog/prohibitedItems/ValuableThings.webp)

- **Cash, coins, credit/debit cards**: Prohibited to prevent theft and fraud.
- **Jewelry exceeding customs value limits**: Requires declaration and insurance; often better shipped via specialized courier services.
- **Passports, IDs, legal documents**: Risk of loss or misuse; generally discouraged for parcel forwarding.

--

### ❌ Tobacco, Alcohol, and Similar Items

- **Cigarettes, cigars, e-cigarettes, vaping liquids**: Subject to excise taxes and import restrictions; often banned in international parcels.
- **Alcoholic beverages (spirits, wine, beer)**: Require licenses and are limited by quantity; many carriers disallow shipping alcohol altogether.
- **Chewing tobacco, snuff**: Regulated and often prohibited.

--

### ❌ Dangerous or Regulated Goods

- **Knives, stun guns, pepper spray, batons**: Considered weapons; require permits or are banned.
- **Firearms, ammunition, replicas**: Strictly prohibited in international mail and courier shipments.
- **Explosives, radioactive, infectious substances**: Forbidden due to extreme hazard.

## What Happens If You Ship a Prohibited Item?

- Customs may **reject or confiscate** the parcel.
- Parcels can be **returned to sender** at high cost or destroyed.
- You may face **legal penalties, fines, or shipment delays**.
- Carrier liability is often limited or excluded for prohibited goods.


## Safe Alternatives and Best Practices

- **Consult your parcel forwarding provider** for pre-shipment checks; many offer free verification services.
- **Use proper labeling and packaging** compliant with international standards (IATA, ADR).
- **Avoid risky categories on first shipments** until you confirm regulations.
- **Obtain necessary permits and licenses** for controlled goods.
- **Check destination country customs rules** thoroughly.
